Stay at JMK Regency – Your Gateway to Varanasi's Spiritual Essence

Located in the heart of Varanasi, JMK Regency is perfectly positioned to immerse you in the city’s rich culture and spirituality. Just minutes away from iconic landmarks like the Kashi Vishwanath Temple (Golden Temple) and the serene Dashashwamedh Ghat, our hotel offers easy access to Varanasi's most revered destinations.

Explore the historic Ramnagar Fort with its fascinating museum of royal artifacts, or visit the intricate Tulsi Manas Temple, crafted from white marble and steeped in history. For those seeking a spiritual connection, the Main Ghats along the holy Ganges River are less than three miles away, making it convenient to witness the breathtaking Ganga Aarti or take a purifying dip in its sacred waters.

With Lal Bahadur Shastri International Airport just 45 minutes away, JMK Regency is the perfect starting point for your Varanasi journey. Experience unmatched hospitality and make your visit to this spiritual city truly memorable with us.

Things to do in Varanasi

7.1 km from the hotel

Banaras Hindu University

North India's oldest and largest university, Banaras Hindu offers excellent academics surrounded by impressive architecture and serene landscapes.

7.5 km from the hotel

Tulsi Manas Temple

Made of white marble in 1964, this temple lies on the site where the epic of Ramayana was composed.

9 km from the hotel

Sarnath

Buddhists believe that after achieving enlightenment, Buddha provided teaching to others at this location, now a popular pilgrimage site.

10 km from the hotel

Ramnagar Fort

Built in the 1700s, this historical fort now houses a museum with vintage American cars, royal jewels, and elephant saddles carved from silver.

7 km from the hotel

Main Ghats at River Ganges

Pilgrims flock to the River Ganges to bathe in the hope of achieving spiritual cleansing.

2 km from the hotel

Kashi Vishwanath Temple

Dedicated to Shiva, the exquisite Kashi Vishwanath Temple is also known as the Golden Temple and draws millions of visitors from around the world each year.
